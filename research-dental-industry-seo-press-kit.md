### Medical/Health Content Best Practices

#### Authoritative Health Sources to Reference
- **CDC Oral Health**: https://www.cdc.gov/oral-health/
- **NIH National Library of Medicine**: https://www.nlm.nih.gov/
- **MedlinePlus**: https://medlineplus.gov/
- **Mayo Clinic**: https://www.mayoclinic.org/
- **Cleveland Clinic**: https://my.clevelandclinic.org/

#### Key Requirements for Health Content
1. **Expert Review**: All content must be reviewed by licensed healthcare professionals
2. **Transparent Attribution**: Clear author names, credentials, professional profile links
3. **Medical Disclaimer**: Visible disclaimers that content is for informational purposes
4. **Evidence-Based**: Reference peer-reviewed studies, clinical trials, established guidelines
5. **Regular Updates**: Content must reflect current medical knowledge (last updated timestamps)
6. **No Medical Advice**: Frame as educational information, never as diagnosis or treatment recommendation
7. **Citation Links**: Link to authoritative sources for all health claims

### Press Kit Must-Haves

#### Essential Elements for Health Tech Startups
1. **Company Overview**
   - One-sentence value proposition (under 10 seconds to understand)
   - Mission and vision statements
   - Founded date and funding status
   - Key metrics (users, providers, locations)

2. **Leadership Team**
   - Founder/CEO biography with credentials
   - Medical/clinical advisors with licenses
   - Professional photos (high-resolution)
   - LinkedIn profile links

3. **Product Information**
   - Product descriptions and technical specifications
   - Clinical validation data and study results
   - FDA/Regulatory approval status (if applicable)
   - High-resolution product images and screenshots

4. **Brand Assets**
   - Logo in multiple formats (PNG, SVG, AI, EPS)
   - Brand guidelines (colors, typography, usage rules)
   - Product photography
   - Team photos
   - Event and award photos

5. **Press Coverage**
   - Recent press releases
   - "Featured in" media logos
   - Industry awards and recognitions
   - Speaking engagements and conference appearances

6. **Clinical Evidence** (Health Tech Specific)
   - Published studies in peer-reviewed journals
   - Clinical trial results and outcomes
   - FDA clearance documentation (public portions)
   - Advisory board credentials

7. **Contact Information**
   - Dedicated PR contact (email, phone)
   - Crisis communication contact
   - Social media handles
   - Newsletter signup

#### Health Tech Specific Considerations
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Include FDA/EMA clearance status where applicable
- **Clinical Validation**: Highlight published studies and clinical trial data
- **HIPAA Compliance**: If handling patient data, include privacy certifications
- **Medical Advisors**: Showcase advisory board with licensed professionals
- **Evidence-Based Claims**: All product claims backed by clinical data

### What Journalists Expect

#### Quick Access Requirements
- **Immediate Download**: One-click access to all assets (no forms required)
- **High-Resolution Images**: 300 DPI minimum for print
- **Multiple Formats**: PNG, JPEG, SVG for logos
- **Recent Content**: Last updated date visible (within 3-6 months)
- **Clear Contact**: Direct PR contact, not generic info email
- **Story Hooks**: Newsworthy angles and trends position
- **Visual Assets**: Infographics, charts, product shots ready for publication
- **Social Proof**: Customer testimonials, case studies, user statistics

#### Common Press Kit Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Outdated Information**: Old team photos, outdated metrics
2. **Complex Navigation**: Buried on website, hard to find
3. **Large File Sizes**: Unoptimized images that are slow to download
4. **Missing Credentials**: No author backgrounds or advisor qualifications
5. **Generic Messaging**: Unclear value proposition
6. **No Context**: Product specs without problem/solution explanation
7. **Dead Links**: Broken image downloads or outdated press releases

## SUMMARY & ACTION ITEMS

### For Dental Guide Content Creation

1. **Data Sources to Reference**
   - Cite ADA MouthHealthy for consumer information
   - Reference NIDCR for government authority
   - Link to specific dental school clinics as examples
   - Use FAIR Health for cost estimates

2. **Cost Data to Include**
   - Regional cost variations from CDC data
   - Average procedure costs from FAIR Health database
   - Income-based disparities from CDC Health Equity data
   - Dental school vs private practice cost comparisons

3. **SEO Requirements to Meet**
   - **E-E-A-T**: Demonstrate experience (case studies, user stories), expertise (cite dentists/researchers), authoritativeness (link to peer-reviewed sources), trustworthiness (clear medical disclaimers, transparent sourcing)
   - **YMYL Compliance**: All health content must be reviewed by dental professionals, include clear author credentials, avoid medical advice (frame as educational)
   - **Quality Signals**: Regular content updates, peer-reviewed citations, HTTPS secure site, clear About page with team credentials

4. **Press Kit Components to Prepare**
   - Company overview with funding and metrics
   - Founder/team bios with credentials
   - Brand guidelines and logo assets
   - Clinical validation data
   - Press coverage and media mentions
   - Clear PR contact information

### Recommended Content Strategy

1. **Credibility Building**
   - Interview practicing dentists for quotes and insights
   - Partner with dental schools for educational content
   - Reference CDC/ADA statistics in data-driven articles
   - Include cost calculators using FAIR Health ranges

2. **Trust Signals**
   - "Last Updated" dates on all health/financial content
   - Author bios with DDS/DMD credentials
   - Medical disclaimer on all pages
   - Sources cited with live links

3. **Differentiation**
   - Focus on dental school clinic alternatives (cost savings)
   - Regional cost comparisons using FAIR Health data
   - Real patient stories and testimonials (with consent)
   - Practical guides based on ADA resources
